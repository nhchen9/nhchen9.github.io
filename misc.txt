<div class="Concepts"> 
    <ul>
      <li>
        Central Limit Theorem - a large enough sample size of data converges to the true distribution.  we can learn and extrapolate from finite experience to reason about future, unknown events.
      </li>
      <li>
        Bayes Theorem/MAP - Updating prior beliefs based on new evidence
      </li>
      <li>
        Efficient Market Hypothesis - The smartest experts in the world obtain approximately optimal solutions which represent an accumulation of contemporary human knowledge; to beat them you either need to get lucky or have inside info.
        More generally, all decision making is an inevitable prediction game of its own.  Disagreeing with the crowd probably means you're wrong, but occasionally it you've discovered something new and valuable.
      </li>
      <li>
        Free Will - Moral beliefs can be reduced down to "because I want it to be so".  People are simply optimizing for utility based on their own values.  
        With enough backpropagation, these values are instilled by forces outside of our control - our parents, friends, teachers, media, and socialization.  
        While we are physically capable of executing any action imaginable, we end up selecting just one.  Is the selection process really our own?
      </li>
    </ul>

  </div>
